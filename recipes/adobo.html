ADOBO

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish known for its savory and tangy flavor, typically featuring chicken or pork braised in a sauce of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and peppercorns. Other common ingredients include bay leaves, sugar, and sometimes oyster sauce. The cooking process involves simmering the meat in the sauce until tender and the sauce has reduced.

Ingredients:

  • Meat: Chicken (thighs, drumsticks, or a combination) or pork (belly, shoulder, or a combination)
  • Liquid: Vinegar (cane vinegar is traditional, but white vinegar works), soy sauce
  • Aromatics: Garlic (minced or crushed), peppercorns (whole black peppercorns are traditional), bay leaves
  • Optional additions: Sugar (brown sugar is common), oyster sauce, onions

Procedure:

  1. Marinate (optional): Combine the meat with soy sauce, garlic, and peppercorns, and marinate for at least 30 minutes (or longer for more flavor).
  2. Sear (optional): Brown the meat in a pot with a little oil to develop flavor and color.
  3. Simmer: Add the remaining ingredients (vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, peppercorns, bay leaves, sugar, etc.) to the pot.
  4. Cover and cook: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer until the meat is tender (40-60 minutes or longer, depending on the meat and size of pieces).
  5. Reduce the sauce: Remove the lid and simmer for a further period (15-30 minutes) to reduce the sauce to your desired thickness.
  6. Adjust seasoning: Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt or more soy sauce or vinegar as needed.
  7. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice.
