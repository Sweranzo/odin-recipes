SINIGANG

Sinigang is a Filipino soup known for its sour and savory broth, typically made with meat or seafood and a variety of vegetables. Common ingredients include tamarind (or a mix like Knorr Sinigang sa Sampalok), meat (pork, beef, fish, or shrimp), tomatoes, onions, radish, string beans, okra, and leafy greens like kangkong. The preparation involves boiling the meat, adding the vegetables and seasonings, and simmering until tender and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • Protein: Pork (belly or ribs), beef, fish (like bangus), or shrimp.
  • Sour Broth Base: Tamarind (fresh or paste), or a Sinigang sa Sampalok mix (like Knorr).
  • Vegetables: Tomatoes, onions, radish (labanos), string beans (sitaw), okra, eggplant, and leafy greens like kangkong (water spinach) or spinach.
  • Seasonings: Fish sauce (patis), salt, and pepper.
  • Optional: Gabi (taro), chili peppers for added spice.

Procedure:

  1. Boil the meat: Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the meat (pork, beef, or fish), and simmer until tender.
  2. Add aromatics: Add onions and tomatoes to the pot and simmer for a few minutes.
  3. ntroduce the souring agent: Add the tamarind (either fresh or in a mix) and let it simmer.
  4. Incorporate vegetables: Add the radish, string beans, eggplant, and okra, and simmer until tender.
  5. Season and add leafy greens: Season with fish sauce and pepper, then add the kangkong or spinach.
  6. Simmer and serve: Let the soup simmer for a few more minutes, then serve hot with rice.
